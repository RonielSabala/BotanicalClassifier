Q: What is the Botanical Garden?
A: A public institution of a scientific, educational, and recreational nature that provides important environmental services.


Q: What is the nature of the botanical garden?
A: The primary nature of the garden is scientific and focused on the conservation of national flora.


Q: What functions does the botanical garden perform?
A: Research, conservation, environmental education, and recreation.


Q: Why are scientific investigations important?
A: Because they allow understanding the nature and characteristics of plants, and with the information produced, they enable sustainable management of floral resources and the environment in general.


Q: What actions does the botanical garden carry out for conservation?
A: Studying the conservation status of Dominican flora, collecting and conserving seeds, reproducing plants in nurseries, supporting forestry projects and programs, training personnel in landscaping and gardening, among others.


Q: What is the importance of environmental education?
A: It helps create awareness among people about the need to care for and manage the environment in a sustainable and friendly way.


Q: What actions does the botanical garden carry out to contribute to environmental education?
A: Courses, workshops, symposiums, and conferences are conducted.


Q: What are the organizational units that make up the institution?
A: The institution has a general directorate and six departments: Administration, Human Resources, Botany, Horticulture, Planning, and Environmental Education.


Q: Of the services offered, which are the most requested?
A:
- Plant identification.
- Social outreach.
- Scientific research advisory services.
- Train tours.
- Herbarium tours.
- Among others.


Q: What days is the botanical garden open to the public and during what hours?
A: From Monday to Sunday, from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.
